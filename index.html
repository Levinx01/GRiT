GRiT: A System for Grounded and Real-time Multi-Humanoid Teleoperation from Vision

Code

GRiT enables real-time multi-humanoid teleoperation with grounded control.

Abstract

We present GRiT, a novel pipeline for Grounded Real-time Multi-Humanoid Teleoperation from Vision. Our work addresses the critical need for a low-cost, universal whole-body teleoperation system capable of controlling multiple humanoid robots simultaneously. At its core, GRiT deploys a marker-less multi-RGB real-time capture system that robustly recovers full-body kinematics with high root-position accuracy.

This is seamlessly paired with a universal motion-tracking controller designed to retarget poses to any Unitree G1 platform, critically preserving both balance and contact during dynamic movements. The system's innovative design ensures that robot movements are "grounded" in physical reality, avoiding common teleoperation artifacts like foot slippage or unnatural floating.

Our framework inherently generalises to multi-user control out-of-the-box, allowing multiple operators to intuitively control their respective humanoids. Furthermore, GRiT serves as a scalable data-collection tool for future multi-robot collaboration studies, offering a practical and cost-effective solution for advancing embodied AI research.
